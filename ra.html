"Resident Assistant Communication Design"

Role: Resident Assistant (Graphic Designer)
Tools: Illustrator, After Effects, Photoshop, Canva

As a Resident Assistant, I have built visuals for event promotions, infographics, newsletters, and social media. Across these various capacities, I have used a variety of applications, styles, and techniques in order to produce graphics. Below are a few samples of work that I have done during my time as an RA.

Pieces of Our History

Pieces of Our History was an interactive passive event built to showcase aspects of Black History. Residents were presented with 9 infographics around the building, each one having a call to action that led to either a task, an article, a piece of code, or information on how to be more politically active. Each resident that completed a prompt was entered into a raffle with three prizes, incentivizing them to engage with the graphics. Below are samples taken from the event.

K-pop Night

K-Pop Night was a collaborative event between three diffrent dorms, and I served as the marketing coordinator and graphic designer for the event. K-Pop Night was done in two iterations during 2024, and both times, I designed flyers to look very similar to various album covers from popular K-Pop groups. The flyer designs were replicated as close as possible, and made in various dimensions to promote through print marketing and social media. Below are samples of promotional materials from the event (with the original album covers on the side for reference).

Both iterations of the event were successful, garnishing over 100+ attendees in total.

MTA Trivia Night

To help first year New York University students acclimate to the transit systems in NYC, I hosted an MTA Trivia Night event, allowing both new NYC residents and real New Yorkers compete on who knows NYC transit the best. The event featured both print and social media marketing with various purposes:

  • A standard flyer to promote the event across social media, emails, and on bulletin boards.
  • Signs for the 6th floor leading incoming residents to the correct tower they live in (while simultaneously promoting the event).
  • Various flyers for each lounge in the residence hall mimicking MTA service change flyers to add a level of familiarity.
